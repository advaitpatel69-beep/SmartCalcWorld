01

🇮🇳 What Is PPF?

Public Provident Fund is a government-backed long-term savings scheme designed for patient wealth accumulation. Unlike market-linked products, PPF has no exposure to equity volatility — the interest rate is declared by the government, and your principal is fully protected. The standard maturity period is 15 years, which naturally enforces discipline and makes it especially powerful when used as the safe-compounding anchor of a broader financial plan.

People use PPF for retirement preparation, children's education funding, building emergency backup savings, and conservative long-term wealth creation. If you are the kind of investor who loses sleep over market swings, PPF's structure is likely to feel calmer and more manageable than equity-heavy products — while still delivering meaningful compounding growth over time.

02

💰 How PPF Interest Works

The most important concept in PPF is annual compounding. Your balance does not just grow because you keep adding money. It grows because last year's balance earns interest this year — and then that larger balance earns interest next year. The basic formula is:

Balancenext = Balancecurrent × (1 + r) + yearly contribution

Every rupee you add joins this compounding engine. Old money compounds; new money starts compounding. When you select monthly simulation mode, the calculator splits your yearly amount into 12 equal deposits and tracks interest through the year before crediting it annually — which more closely mirrors how most people actually contribute.

Timing also matters within the year. Money deposited earlier earns more days of interest than money deposited later. That is why monthly discipline can meaningfully outperform a single year-end lump-sum deposit over a 15-year horizon, even when the total annual amount is identical.

05

🔄 What Happens After 15 Years?

This is one of the most underutilised parts of PPF planning. If you do not urgently need the money at maturity, extending in 5-year blocks is frequently the better wealth-building decision. Here is the key insight: the early years of compounding are slower because the balance is small. Later years feel dramatically more rewarding because the balance is large — and a large balance multiplied by even a stable rate produces sizeable annual interest.

The real compounding magic in PPF often shows up after year 15, not before it. Stopping at maturity is a choice — not a requirement.

An account that has grown to ₹40 lakh at year 15 might add ₹2.8 lakh or more in interest in its first year of extension alone — far more than the early years of the account produced. That scale effect is why this calculator's extension mode is one of its most valuable planning tools.

06

📊 Why Scenario Comparison Changes How You Plan

A single projection is a starting point. Two projections are a conversation. The comparison mode lets you test ₹1.5 lakh versus ₹1.2 lakh, or 7.1% versus 7.6%, and see the full 15-year difference in one view. These numbers are not abstract — they represent real planning choices about what you can commit to and how sensitive the outcome is to each variable.

This is more realistic planning because life rarely follows one perfect path. In some years income rises and contributions can increase. In others, savings rates tighten. A comparison view does not predict the future, but it prepares you to think clearly about different possible futures — which is what good financial planning is really about.

07

🚀 How to Use This Calculator Effectively

Start with an amount you can commit to comfortably — not optimistically. Use monthly mode if your contributions are spread across the year, or yearly mode if you invest in one annual deposit. If you expect income to grow over time, add a small contribution growth rate and watch the projection improve. Then explore extension mode to see what staying invested beyond 15 years could mean for your final corpus.

Finally, open the comparison panel. Test one lower and one higher contribution scenario. Look at the result cards, the yearly growth chart, and the year-by-year table together. The combination of those views makes the compounding story far more vivid than any single summary number. PPF works best when you understand it deeply — this calculator is designed to help you do exactly that.

Frequently Asked Questions

Public Provident Fund is a long-term savings scheme backed by the Government of India. It is known for stable compounding, a 15-year maturity term, and consistently strong popularity among conservative savers who prioritise capital safety over market-linked returns.

PPF interest rates are revised quarterly by the government and can change. This calculator loads 7.1% as the default reference because the official National Savings Institute table listed 7.1% through March 31, 2026 for FY 2025–26. Always verify the latest notification before making planning decisions.

Interest is credited annually at the end of each financial year. In this calculator, yearly mode uses the annual compounding formula directly, while monthly mode splits the yearly amount into 12 equal deposits, accrues interest on the running monthly balance, and still credits the full year's interest at year-end — closely mirroring real PPF deposit behaviour.

Yes. Many investors contribute to their PPF account on a monthly basis throughout the year, which mirrors a salary-linked savings habit. This calculator's monthly simulation mode is designed exactly for that use case and shows you how spreading deposits across the year changes the yearly balance path compared to a single annual deposit.

After the initial 15-year maturity, a PPF account can be extended in blocks of 5 years with or without further contributions. This is often one of the most financially powerful decisions an account holder can make, because the large corpus built over 15 years then compounds at an even larger scale. The extension module in this calculator lets you project exactly how much additional value those extra years could create.

PPF is widely known for favourable tax treatment under the applicable Indian income tax rules, covering contributions, earned interest, and maturity proceeds. Tax laws can change, so it is prudent to confirm the current position with a chartered accountant or tax professional before making decisions based on tax treatment.

PPF is intentionally designed as a long-term product, so early full withdrawal is not permitted. Partial withdrawals are allowed under specific conditions after the 7th year. Loans against the PPF balance are also available in certain years. This calculator focuses on long-term maturity and extension planning rather than partial withdrawal timing, so always check current PPF rules for withdrawal-specific guidance.

They are fundamentally different products solving different problems. PPF offers government-backed safety, predictable compounding, and tax efficiency. Mutual funds are market-linked and can potentially deliver higher returns over long periods, but they also come with volatility, no capital guarantee, and returns that depend entirely on market performance. Many serious investors use both — PPF as the safe core and mutual funds for growth — rather than choosing one exclusively.

That comparison gives you a realistic range of outcomes rather than a single number — which makes your long-term planning more robust and honest.
